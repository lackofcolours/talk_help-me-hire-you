Recruitment process

What does recruitment process generally looks like?

From my POV, there are 6 stages

Phase 1: Preparing

  • Why hire an intern or junior talent?
  • Which team?
  • What does that team need?

‚úÖ Job description/ad

Organisation asks itself, why hire an intern or junior talent?

Depending on its size, business life cycle, and values, answers can range from:

  • Questionable "save cost"
  • Expanding rapidly, not enough talent in market
  • Train into niche specialities like ecommerce and Salesforce
  • Lofty "fostering future generation of coders"
  • (intern) Entice them into future role with us

This will inform job posting tones and attitudes.

Which team?

For org with multiple teams, sometimes teams can volunteer or request.

Team's culture and natuer of work will inform the "What you'll learn" section from the ad.

Team needs

  • Team is full of disgruntled old people and keen on fresh young energy
  • Members' growth plans involve practice mentoring
  • Team practice/refine onboarding process
  • Team has regular/rotation spot for junior talent
  • (intern) Temporary help to get over a feature release

Informs "What you'll do" section

Phase 3: Screening

  • Reactive - Candidates apply
  • Proactive - Search on a platform
  • Both

2,000+ profiles (~57profiles/hour) => 25-100 per role

Use SoT filters, profiles, CV, and portfolio

‚úÖ Narrowed pool of candidates

3 main ways

Well known companies with track record will adopt a reactive strategy. Just screen from people who applied.

So if you never registered interest or reached out, you won't be considered.

Lesser known companies understand that if candidates don't know about them, no one would apply.

So they'd actively reach out, and shortlist candidates.

Organisations with a bit of dedicated resource (perosonnel, time, and $$) may adopt both

Say there are 2,000+ candidates on SoT

Say you're given 1 week (35hrs) to narrow down the pool for teams.

If I'm a proactive type, I'll be trying to sort through 57 profiles per hour

Which I'll narrow down manageable ratio of around 25-100:1 role

What helps me decide?

Student status (penultimate, last year, etc) filter, filled out profiles, well written CV keyword which matched the job description/team needs, and spreadsheets.

Empty or no effort profile, CV, and lack of portfolio could be deadly

Due to time and resource constraints, candidates become numbers and spreadsheets

After a week's of hard work, I now have a much smaller list to present to my teams

Phase 4: Selecting

  • Most resource heavy - Team activity
  • Most intense period - Short, and very busy
    • Shortlist candidates
    • Technical assessment (optional)
    • Interviews
